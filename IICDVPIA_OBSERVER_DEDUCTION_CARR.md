## The Deduction

### Step 1: Two Dimensions Interfere

Two dimension vectors (DV₁, DV₂) interfere. Under conservation, total entropy K redistributes. The entropy tensor develops one off-diagonal term: S₁₂. This off-diagonal term IS the coalescence product.

Before: S₁₁ + S₂₂ = K (diagonal, no coupling)
After: S₁₁' + S₂₂' + 2S₁₂ = K (coupled, but total unchanged)

The coalescence product S₁₂ exists at the boundary. Neither dimension mixed. Both changed shape.

### Step 2: The Observer Arrives

The observer is not special — the observer is DV₃. Another dimension vector. They bring their own entropy S₃₃ to the system.

The system was: K distributed across 2 dimensions with 1 coupling.
The system becomes: K + S₃₃ distributed across 3 dimensions with 3 couplings (S₁₂, S₁₃, S₂₃).

### Step 3: Conservation Forces Change

Conservation still holds. The new total K' = K + S₃₃ is invariant. But the redistribution pattern *must* change.

Because the observer's dimension vector interferes with both DV₁ and DV₂. New off-diagonal terms appear: S₁₃ (coupling between dimension 1 and observer) and S₂₃ (coupling between dimension 2 and observer).

These new couplings cost entropy — from the redistribution law:

ΔS₁₁ = -(ΔS₁₂ + ΔS₁₃)
ΔS₂₂ = -(ΔS₁₂ + ΔS₂₃)

**The original coupling S₁₂ must change** to accommodate S₁₃ and S₂₃. The total is conserved. The new couplings draw entropy from the diagonal terms *and* from the existing coupling.

**Therefore: the coalescence product the observer sees (S₁₂') is not the coalescence product that existed before observation (S₁₂).**

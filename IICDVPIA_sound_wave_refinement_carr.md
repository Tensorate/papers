## What Changed

The conservation paper (v01) said entropy redistributes *between* the interfering dimensions — A gives to B, B gives to A, off-diagonal terms represent exchange.

This refinement says that's wrong.

**The interfering dimensions don't exchange anything with each other.**

They interfere. They produce a pattern at the boundary. But like sound waves bouncing off a corner in a room, the waves pass *through* each other. They don't trade energy. They don't trade entropy. They remain themselves.

The redistribution happens in the **adiabatic whole** — the room, the full system — not between the waves. Somewhere else in the room, compensating entropic change occurs to balance what appears at the corner. The corner looks hot. Another spot goes cold. The room conserves. But the two waves didn't exchange.
